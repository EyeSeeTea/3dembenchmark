Particle Picking (Demo)

Particle Picking for Cryo-Electron Microscopy

Automatic Particle Picking algorithms are crucial to achieve high resolution in which thousands of particle projections must be combined in 3D. Datasets under this task help to design algorithms with a low number of false positives (wrongly picked particles), a high yield (picking so many particles as possible in each micrograph), and as centered as possible.

Datasets

KLH Dataset I

82 defocus micrographs, coordinate centers of ~1000 side-view particles, a preliminary 3D reconstruction. Acquired at farther from focus (FFF) conditions (~ -3µm).

Images were acquired, using a Philips CM200 TEM equipped with a 2Kx2K CCD Tietz camera, as defocal pairs at a nominal magnification of 66,000x and a voltage of 120 KeV, using the Leginon system (Potter et al., 1999 ; Carragher et al., 2000). Pixel size is 2.2Å at the specimen and the accumulated dose for each high magnification image area was ~10 e/Å².

To obtain a true positive the particle must be picked in a circle of pixels radio around the gold standard coordinates. How to pick?

Ad2 ts1 Dataset

308 micrographs, coordinate centers of ~9.000 particles, a preliminary 3D reconstruction. Images were automatically sorted into defocus groups covering a range between -0.5 and -5.1 µm.

Human adenovirus type 2(Ad2 ts1) samples were dialyzed for 1 hour at 4°C against PBS, applied to freshly carbon-coated, glow discharged Quantifoil R2/4 300 mesh Cu/Rh grids, and vitrified in liquid ethane using a Le ica CPC plunger. Grids were mounted in a Gatan 626 cryostage and examined in a FEI Tecnai G2 FEG microscope operating at 200 kV. Micrographs were recorded on Kodak SO-163 film under low dose conditions at a nominal magnification of 50,000×, and digitized in a Zeiss Photoscan TD scanner using a step size of 7 µm (1.4 Å in the sample).
